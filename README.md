**map_scrapping_alfamart** is a Python-based data scraping and geospatial mapping project designed to collect, process, and visualize Alfamart store location data across Indonesia.

The project automates the extraction of publicly available store information, such as store names, addresses, geographic coordinates, cities, provinces, and other relevant location attributes. The collected data is then cleaned, standardized, and transformed into a structured dataset that can be used for location intelligence, market research, business expansion analysis, competitor mapping, and geospatial visualization.

This project can help users understand the geographical distribution of Alfamart stores, identify areas with high or low store density, analyze regional coverage, and discover potential opportunities for retail expansion or business partnerships. The final dataset can also be integrated with mapping and visualization tools such as Folium, Mapbox, Google Maps, Tableau, Power BI, or other Geographic Information System platforms.

Key capabilities of this project include:

* Automated collection of Alfamart store location data.
* Extraction of store names, addresses, cities, provinces, and coordinates.
* Data cleaning and standardization.
* Duplicate store detection and removal.
* Conversion of raw data into structured CSV or JSON formats.
* Geospatial analysis using latitude and longitude data.
* Interactive store mapping and location visualization.
* Store density and regional coverage analysis.
* Preparation of datasets for business intelligence and market research.
* Support for further integration with external demographic or commercial datasets.

The project is useful for data analysts, researchers, developers, retail businesses, logistics companies, property analysts, and anyone interested in understanding the distribution of convenience stores in Indonesia.

This repository is intended for educational, analytical, and research purposes. Users are responsible for ensuring that data collection activities comply with the applicable website terms of service, robots.txt policies, privacy requirements, and local regulations.
